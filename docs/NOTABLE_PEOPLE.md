# Notable People

> Scientists, chemists, and physicists whose lives and work shaped our understanding of elements and matter. Each one is a potential character, card, or story in the game.

---

**Dmitri Mendeleev** — Russian chemist who created the periodic table in 1869 and predicted the existence of undiscovered elements with such precision that finding them proved him right; the ultimate prophet card.

**Marie Curie** — discovered polonium and radium, coined the term "radioactivity," and is the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences (Physics and Chemistry); an obvious legendary character.

**Pierre Curie** — Marie's husband and research partner, co-discovered radioactivity, and died when a horse-drawn cart ran over his head in the rain; a tragic support character.

**Antoine Lavoisier** — the father of modern chemistry who named oxygen and hydrogen, wrote the first modern chemistry textbook, and was guillotined during the French Revolution at 50; law, order, then chaos.

**Ernest Rutherford** — discovered that atoms have a dense nucleus by shooting alpha particles at gold foil and watching them bounce back; changed everything about how we picture the atom.

**Niels Bohr** — developed the first quantum model of the atom (electrons in shells), smuggled out of Nazi-occupied Denmark in a fishing boat, and later worked on the Manhattan Project.

**Lise Meitner** — co-discovered nuclear fission with Otto Hahn but was left out of the Nobel Prize he received for it; one of the greatest scientific injustices of the 20th century; meitnerium is named for her.

**Henry Moseley** — the 26-year-old physicist who reorganized the periodic table by atomic number instead of atomic mass (fixing Mendeleev's few errors), then died in the trenches of WWI at 27; a character whose story ended too soon.

**Glenn T. Seaborg** — co-discovered 10 elements (more than anyone in history), was involved in the Manhattan Project, and had seaborgium named after him while he was still alive; the most element-connected human to ever live.

**Humphry Davy** — isolated sodium, potassium, calcium, magnesium, barium, and boron for the first time using his newly invented electric battery; the greatest element hunter of the 1800s.

**Carl Wilhelm Scheele** — the Swedish pharmacist who discovered oxygen, chlorine, manganese, and several other elements but kept getting scooped on credit because he published slowly; history's most underrated chemist.

**Robert Bunsen** — invented the Bunsen burner, pioneered spectroscopy to identify elements by the light they emit, and discovered cesium and rubidium; the lab itself is his legacy.

**Yuri Oganessian** — the living physicist who pioneered the synthesis of superheavy elements and had oganesson (element 118) named after him; one of only two people to have an element named after them while alive.

**Enrico Fermi** — built the first nuclear reactor in a squash court under a Chicago football stadium in 1942, essentially in secret; the most understated world-changing experiment in history.

**Alfred Nobel** — invented dynamite, made a fortune from it, then upon reading his own premature obituary (which called him "the merchant of death") created the Nobel Prizes to repair his legacy; regret as a character arc.
