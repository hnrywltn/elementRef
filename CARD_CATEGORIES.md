# Card Categories — Element Families

> Each element belongs to one family. Every card in a family shares a passive category trait, plus its own unique ability on top of that. Synergies within families are intentional — deck building should reward leaning into a family.

## Alkali Metals
**Elements:** Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr  
**Real trait:** Extremely reactive, especially with water. Soft, low-density metals. Violently release energy on contact.

**Category Passive — Reactive Burst:**
When an Alkali Metal card attacks, deal 1 splash damage to a random adjacent enemy card.

**Playstyle:** Aggro. These are your face-damage dealers. Low health, high attack, cheap to play. Best used in burst decks that end the game fast. Poor late-game staying power.

**Family Synergy idea:** *Chain Reaction* — if two or more Alkali Metal cards attack in the same turn, each attack after the first deals +1 damage.

**Notable cards:**
- **Francium (Fr, 87)** — Rarest naturally occurring element. Most reactive alkali metal. Legendary-tier despite category being Common.
- **Potassium (K, 19)** — The garbage collector. Ability: *Electrochemical Pulse* — discard an opponent's lowest-cost card from their hand.
- **Sodium (Na, 11)** — Ability: *Saltwater* — when paired with a Halogen card (Cl specifically), both cards get +1/+1.

## Alkaline Earth Metals
**Elements:** Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Ra  
**Real trait:** Less reactive than Alkali metals. Form strong, stable compounds. Structural role in biology (calcium in bones, magnesium in chlorophyll).

**Category Passive — Neutron Shield:**
Enter play with 1 bonus shield (absorbs 1 instance of damage completely, then breaks).

**Playstyle:** Defensive. Cheap, durable blockers. Good early-game walls that let you develop bigger cards safely. Ra is radioactive — see Actinide crossover note.

**Family Synergy idea:** *Structural Support* — while 2+ Alkaline Earth Metal cards are on your board, your other cards gain +1 health.

**Notable cards:**
- **Radium (Ra, 88)** — Alkaline Earth Metal *and* radioactive. Gets both the Neutron Shield passive AND the Decay mechanic. High risk.
- **Calcium (Ca, 20)** — Ability: *Bone Structure* — give all your cards +1 health (permanent).
- **Magnesium (Mg, 12)** — Ability: *Photosynthesis Engine* — draw 1 card (magnesium is the center of chlorophyll).

---

## Transition Metals
**Elements:** Sc through Zn, Y through Cd, Hf through Hg, Rf through Cn (38 total)  
**Real trait:** The most diverse group. Variable oxidation states, many are conductors, catalysts, or have magnetic properties. Includes gold, iron, copper, titanium, platinum.

**Category Passive — Variable Oxidation:**
Each Transition Metal can switch between two modes (defined per card) once per turn. No cost.

**Playstyle:** Versatile. The largest category with the widest range of effects. Best for control and midrange strategies. These cards reward understanding their individual abilities.

**Family Synergy idea:** *Alloying* — when two different Transition Metal cards are on your board, both gain +1 attack (metals strengthen each other when combined).

**Notable cards:**
- **Iron (Fe, 26)** — Mode A: *Steel* (+2 defense). Mode B: *Rust* (deal 1 damage to a random enemy each turn, slow corrosion).
- **Gold (Au, 79)** — Rare. Ability: *Inert Value* — cannot be targeted by opponent abilities. High cost, high health. The ultimate tank.
- **Copper (Cu, 29)** — Ability: *Conductor* — when you play an electron-costing ability, reduce its cost by 1.
- **Titanium (Ti, 22)** — Ability: *Aerospace Grade* — takes half damage from all sources (rounded up).
- **Mercury (Hg, 80)** — Liquid at room temp. Ability: *Liquid Metal* — can move to any board position after attacking.
- **Platinum (Pt, 78)** — Ability: *Catalyst* — reduce the cost of the next Reaction card you play this turn by 3.

## Post-Transition Metals
**Elements:** Al, Ga, In, Sn, Tl, Pb, Bi, Nh, Fl, Mc, Lv (8 stable-ish)  
**Real trait:** Soft, malleable, low melting points. Between transition metals and metalloids in the periodic table. Often considered "weak metals."

**Category Passive — Malleable:**
When played adjacent to any card, copy that card's category passive until the end of the turn.

**Playstyle:** Flexible adapters. These cards are glue — they amplify whatever strategy you're running. A post-transition metal next to an Alkali Metal temporarily becomes aggressive. Next to a Noble Gas, it becomes a utility piece.

**Notable cards:**
- **Lead (Pb, 82)** — Ability: *Heavy Shielding* — reduce all damage your hero takes this turn by 3 (lead radiation shielding).
- **Aluminum (Al, 13)** — Ability: *Lightweight Frame* — costs 1 less electron for every other metal card in your hand.
- **Bismuth (Bi, 83)** — Ability: *Crystallize* — lock a target card in place; it cannot attack for 1 turn (bismuth forms beautiful crystal structures).

---

## Metalloids
**Elements:** B, Si, Ge, As, Sb, Te, At (sometimes Po)  
**Real trait:** Semiconductors — properties between metals and nonmetals. Conduct electricity under some conditions. Silicon is the backbone of computing.

**Category Passive — Semiconductor:**
At the start of your turn, choose: this card acts as a Metal (gains +1 attack) or a Nonmetal (gains +1 defense) until your next turn.

**Playstyle:** Tempo. These cards are efficient if you're reading the board correctly. Choosing the wrong mode at the wrong time wastes their value.

**Notable cards:**
- **Silicon (Si, 14)** — Ability: *Microprocessor* — look at the top 3 cards of your deck, rearrange in any order.
- **Arsenic (As, 33)** — Ability: *Poison* — deals 1 damage to the opponent at the end of each turn it's on the board.
- **Boron (B, 5)** — Ability: *Borosilicate* — when paired with Silicon, both cards gain +2/+2.

---

## Reactive Nonmetals
**Elements:** H, C, N, O, P, S, Se  
**Real trait:** Diverse group. Most are gases. Form the basis of organic chemistry and life. Highly reactive in different ways.

**Category Passive — Reactive:**
Abilities trigger off opponent actions rather than your own. (e.g., "When the opponent plays a card, deal 1 damage to it.")

**Playstyle:** Counter-play and disruption. These cards punish the opponent for acting. Good against aggressive strategies.

**Notable cards:**
- **Oxygen (O, 8)** — Ability: *Oxidation* — deal 2 damage to any Metal card. Game mechanic: metals rust when hit by Oxygen.
- **Carbon (C, 6)** — Ability: *Allotropes* — has 3 different forms: Graphite (card draw), Diamond (gain 3 defense), Fullerene (deal 1 to all enemies). Choose when played.
- **Hydrogen (H, 1)** — Cheapest card in the game (1 electron). Ability: *Fusion* — if two Hydrogen cards attack the same target, deal double damage and create a Helium card in your hand.
- **Nitrogen (N, 7)** — Ability: *Inert Atmosphere* — prevent all damage to your cards this turn (nitrogen is used to create inert atmospheres).
- **Sulfur (S, 16)** — Ability: *Vulcanize* — give a target card +2 attack permanently (sulfur strengthens rubber via vulcanization).

## Halogens
**Elements:** F, Cl, Br, I, At, Ts  
**Real trait:** Most reactive nonmetals. Always trying to gain 1 electron to complete their outer shell. Form salts when they react with metals. Corrosive.

**Category Passive — Corrosive:**
Any card a Halogen attacks gains a Corrode counter. Cards with a Corrode counter take +1 damage from all sources.

**Playstyle:** Attrition. Halogens are better the longer the game goes. They degrade opponent cards over time. Pair with slow/defensive strategies to let corrosion stack up.

**Family Synergy idea:** *Salt Formation* — when a Halogen card and an Alkali Metal card are on your board at the same time, both gain +2/+2 (they naturally react to form stable salts).

**Notable cards:**
- **Fluorine (F, 9)** — Most reactive element in the game. Ability: *Acid Etch* — apply 2 Corrode counters instead of 1 on attack.
- **Chlorine (Cl, 17)** — Ability: *Disinfect* — remove all ability buffs from a target card.
- **Iodine (I, 53)** — Ability: *Tracer* — for 2 turns, see all cards your opponent draws.

---

## Noble Gases
**Elements:** He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn  
**Real trait:** Full outer electron shells. Don't react with anything. Stable, inert.

**Category Passive — Inert:**
Cannot be targeted by opponent card abilities. (Can still take combat damage from direct attacks.)

**Playstyle:** Utility and control. No Noble Gas is meant to win a fight — they provide board advantages, shields, card draw, and disruption. The hardest category to counter because abilities can't touch them.

> See GAME_DESIGN.md for individual Noble Gas abilities and their role as a design escape valve.

**Notable cards:**
- **Helium (He, 2)** — Ability: *Levitate* — one of your cards cannot be attacked this turn.
- **Neon (Ne, 10)** — Ability: *Illuminate* — reveal opponent's full hand for 1 turn.
- **Argon (Ar, 18)** — Ability: *Inert Shell* — give any card +3 defense until end of turn.
- **Krypton (Kr, 36)** — Ability: *Kryptonite* — reduce target card's attack to 0 for 1 turn.
- **Xenon (Xe, 54)** — Ability: *Anesthetic* — target card cannot attack next turn.
- **Radon (Rn, 86)** — Only Noble Gas with Decay (radioactive). Ability: *Invisible Poison* — deal 1 damage to opponent hero each turn. Decay counter: 4.

## Lanthanides (Rare Earth Metals)
**Elements:** La, Ce, Pr, Nd, Pm, Sm, Eu, Gd, Tb, Dy, Ho, Er, Tm, Yb, Lu  
**Real trait:** Rare earth elements with strong magnetic and luminescent properties. Used in magnets, lasers, and electronics. Hard to separate from each other.

**Category Passive — Magnetic:**
When played, pull one opponent card from their hand at random and reveal it (but do not steal it). Magnetic attraction to information.

**Playstyle:** High-power combo. Expensive, but each Lanthanide does something dramatic. Their individual abilities draw on real-world uses: magnets, lasers, MRI contrast agents, night-vision tech.

**Notable cards:**
- **Neodymium (Nd, 60)** — Ability: *Neodymium Magnet* — pull a card from the opponent's board to your side for 1 turn.
- **Europium (Eu, 63)** — Ability: *Luminescence* — deal 2 damage to all opponent cards (Eu is in fluorescent lights).
- **Gadolinium (Gd, 64)** — Ability: *MRI Contrast* — see the opponent's full deck order for 1 turn.
- **Promethium (Pm, 61)** — Only radioactive Lanthanide. Gets Decay mechanic. Ability: *Nuclear Battery* — gain 3 electrons this turn only.

## Actinides (Radioactive)
**Elements:** Ac, Th, Pa, U, Np, Pu, Am, Cm, Bk, Cf, Es, Fm, Md, No, Lr  
**Real trait:** All radioactive. Heaviest naturally occurring elements. Uranium and Plutonium are famous for nuclear energy/weapons. Most are synthetic.

**Category Passive — Radioactive Decay:**
All Actinide cards have a Decay Counter (set per card, typically 3–5). At the end of each of your turns, the counter drops by 1. When it hits 0, the card either dies or transforms into its decay product (a weaker card).

**Playstyle:** Nuclear aggro. These are the most powerful cards in the game but come with the cost of impermanence. Best used to swing the game state dramatically in a short window. Poor in slow games where the decay timer runs out before you can capitalize.

**Decay chain idea:** U → Th → Pa → Ac → ... (each decay produces the next element — could be a mechanic where Actinide cards create weaker Actinide cards as they decay)

**Notable cards:**
- **Uranium (U, 92)** — Ability: *Fission* — deal 5 damage split across all opponent cards. Decay: 4.
- **Plutonium (Pu, 94)** — Ability: *Critical Mass* — if 2 Plutonium cards are on your board at the same time, deal 8 damage to the opponent hero immediately. Decay: 3.
- **Americium (Am, 95)** — Ability: *Smoke Detector* — when an opponent card is played, deal 1 damage to the opponent hero. Decay: 5. (Am-241 is in smoke detectors)
- **Curium (Cm, 96)** — Ability: *Heat Source* — restore 2 HP to your hero each turn. Decay: 4. (Cm powers spacecraft RTGs)

## Synthetic / Superheavy (Legendary)
**Elements:** Nh (113), Fl (114), Mc (115), Lv (116), Ts (117), Og (118)  
**Real trait:** Synthesized only in particle accelerators. Exist for milliseconds. No confirmed chemical properties. Pure physics.

**Category Passive — Unstable Existence:**
Synthetic cards do not have a Decay Counter. Instead, they have a **Probability Counter** — each turn, roll a virtual die. On a 1, the card vanishes (returns to your hand at reduced cost). High risk, high ceiling.

**Playstyle:** Legendary wildcards. Each one is a completely unique effect that doesn't follow normal rules. Only one of each can be in a deck.

**Notable cards:**
- **Oganesson (Og, 118)** — The end of the periodic table. Ability: *Table Complete* — if all 6 Noble Gases are on your board, instantly win the game. (Og is technically a Noble Gas but is so heavy it may not be inert.)
- **Tennessine (Ts, 117)** — Ability: *Half-Life Gambit* — flip a coin. Heads: destroy the most powerful card on the board. Tails: destroy your most powerful card.
